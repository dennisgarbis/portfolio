Creating a Landing Page

We've all seen landing pages. They try to get you to sign up for what the business is selling. It could be a free trial for Netflix, for example. Landing pages usually do not have any external links. They try to convert a visitor by having them use the call to action button. Do they work? Sometimes I would say. Does mine?

How it Started

This assignment had us focusing on creating a landing page for a business. The project was to include three call-to-action buttons, a CSS animated submit button, a form field, and a video banner. I created a wireframe using Abode InDesign and then used PhotoShop to assemble multiple drafts for my ideal landing page.


I didn't want the hero banner to be entirely a video. I was looking to share the space with either a form field or an introduction to the business. I tried multiple colors; red, purple, and black. The final draft was all black with some yellow to match the logo.

Proposals

Before creating a landing page for a business, we had to develop a written proposal. The proposal included the purpose of the project. How did the client benefit from this project, and what was the scope of the project. Also included was a description of the intended target audience. The final part of the proposal was to describe the design and any messages I would like to communicate.


The storyboard explains to the client how the banner video would play out. Screenshots were used, with a description underneath telling the client what was happening during the scene and how long it would last. I had never created an animation or edited any video before this class, so this was a first. I enjoyed putting it together, trying different techniques, and seeing how it turned out was exciting.

I wanted the landing page to have four levels. The hero banner ended up divided between a video and a form field.

Beneath that was a welcome section that included three images. The images were screenshots of the video playing in the hero banner.

Following was another area split in two. One side has a picture of a rack of weights. I photoshopped the logo onto the wall behind the dumbells. Opposite the image, I included the mission statement for the business, which I created.

The testimonial section finished off the landing page. I wanted to showcase a variety of members, so I included a young lady, an older gentleman, and some random guy.

The Finished Product

The project took six weeks from start to finish. Creating and editing the video is where I spent most of my time. I tried many styles, adding different colors, fonts, and videos to get the perfect representation of what my landing page should be. I included the logo in multiple areas of the page to have it jump out to the visitor. This class introduced me to animation, and it is something I also used in future school and personal projects.
