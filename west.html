25 WEST

BREWING CO.

Single-Page Website

A friend of mine opened up a brewery a few years back. It had excellent food and a great atmosphere, but something didn't click. It stayed open for a year and a half but closed its doors right before covid hit. I like to think that my single-page website would've helped keep its doors open had I created it in time. But that wasn't to be. So this is me paying homage to my friends' brewery. The images and videos seen below, besides the hero image, are fictitious and have nothing to do with the original brewery.

Project Scope

The objective of this project was to create a one-page micro-site for a small business. I decided to do a brewery, which I mentioned above. The project details were for the site to be a minimum of four tiers of content, with 3-6 call-to-action blurbs. We had to have a contact form field with an animated submit button, add navigation using anchor links, and add a type of animation to the page.


As you can see, my wireframe was a good start to how my page ended up. I used Photoshop to design the wireframe using images and color, which I then used as a template to code out the site.

Images

The majority of images I used on the site were from free stock photo sites, such as pexels and pixabay. I did find a few pictures online that were from the actual brewery. One was for a beer handle with the logo, and another was a picture of beer kegs.

I also made the logo transparent and placed it on the beer mugs using Photoshop. I had a lot of fun with Photoshop. I have done a few other projects where I used Photoshop and will post a link to it once I update them to my site.

Editing

Have I mentioned how much I enjoyed working with After Effects?

I downloaded a few stock videos of beers from a stock video site. I had them slowly transition into each other with a nice fade. Each video also had its heading, with a different transition shown on the screen. The last part of the video is the image of the two pints with the logo. I took one of the logos and had it animate from the pint glass to cover the entire video.

I also made a video showcasing the food that the brewery offers. I took seven videos and edited them with different transitions to make a smooth video. The last part of the video is again an image, but now I had the logo fade into the picture.

The Finished Product

This project took us 5 weeks to complete. I made sure the page was responsive and that it had four tiers of content. I also added two different modals for the beer and food menu. It was my first attempt at JavaScript and did need some help, but I did change some of the code around to have it do what I wanted. My skills have improved since I made this site, and there might be a few things I would change, but overall I look at it as a good way to show what I can do with editing videos and making a single page website look good.
