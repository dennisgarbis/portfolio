25 WEST

BREWING CO.

Single-Page Website

A friend of mine opened up a brewery a few years back. It had excellent food and a great atmosphere, but something didn't click. It stayed open for a year and a half but closed its doors right before covid hit. I like to think that my single-page website would've helped keep its doors open had I created it in time. But that wasn't to be. So this is me paying homage to my friends' brewery. The images and videos seen below, besides the hero image, are fictitious and have nothing to do with the original brewery.

Project Scope

The objective for this project was to a one page micro-site for a small business. I decided to do a brewery, which I detailed above. The project details were for the site to be a minimum of four tiers of content, with 3-6 call-to-action blurbs. We had to have a contact form field with an animated submit button, add a navigation using anchor links, and also add some kind of animation to the page

I enjoyed creating videos, as seen in my earlier works with the fitness page, so I decided to make a few edited videos. One will be showcasing the food, while the other will be focused on the drinks. I used After Effects for both videos.

The Finished Product

The project took me about a month to finish, working on it several hours a week. I spent the most time adjusting the table showing the hours of operation, the carousel with the food, and the about us section. The sticking point with the carousel was how to have the title of the image displayed on the screen. I learned a lot using Bootstrap. Once I got the hang of how the code works, it was straightforward. Though I enjoy using flexbox and do understand it well, Bootstrap will be something I will be using in the future again.
